# Operator Role — Digital Trader / Commission Agent

Operators are the **digital traders** of OBAOL Supreme. They manage supplier portfolios, activate trading activity, and keep execution moving across the platform. Operators do **not** own products; they **own relationships** and **manage portfolios** on behalf of OBAOL.

## Role Objective
The main objective of the Operator role is to:

- Build strong relationships with supplier associates
- Ensure supplier companies remain active on the platform
- Encourage suppliers to list and maintain products
- Maintain regular communication with suppliers
- Support the creation of real trading opportunities

A strong supplier relationship often leads to:

- More product listings
- Faster responses to market enquiries
- Buyers being referred directly by suppliers
- Stronger long-term trade relationships

## Core Responsibilities
### 1. Supplier Relationship Management
Operators maintain regular communication with suppliers. This includes:

- Calling suppliers regularly
- Understanding supplier needs and concerns
- Building long-term trust with associates
- Acting as a representative of OBAOL in all communications

Important principle:

Conversations should not always focus on making products live.  
Focus on building a genuine business relationship.

Suppliers should feel comfortable communicating with the Operator whenever needed.

### 2. Platform Activity Management
Operators ensure supplier products remain active on the platform. Responsibilities include:

- Ensuring all supplier products are listed
- Keeping product listings active
- Updating product availability
- Monitoring supplier responsiveness to enquiries
- Encouraging suppliers to update their offerings

Example supplier portfolio:

- 10 supplier companies
- 5 products per company
- Total products managed: 50

If trades occur involving these products, the Operator benefits from the commission system.

### 3. Supplier Activation
Operators continuously encourage suppliers to remain active by:

- Adding new products
- Updating product information
- Adjusting pricing when necessary
- Responding quickly to enquiries
- Participating actively in trading opportunities

The more active a supplier becomes, the more trade opportunities are created.

### 4. Market Communication
Operators act as a communication bridge between the market and suppliers:

- Sharing market updates
- Informing suppliers about demand trends
- Communicating buyer requirements
- Helping suppliers position products competitively

This keeps suppliers aligned with real market demand.

## Supplier Portfolio Responsibility (Not Product Ownership)
Each Operator is assigned a portfolio of supplier companies. Portfolio responsibility means:

- Maintaining the supplier relationship
- Managing supplier product listings
- Ensuring supplier activity on the platform

The more suppliers an Operator manages, the larger their trading portfolio becomes.

Example portfolio:

- Suppliers managed: 20
- Products per supplier: 5
- Total products under management: 100

More active suppliers create more opportunities for trading and commission.

## Portfolio Benefits
Portfolio responsibility creates long-term incentives. If a trade occurs involving a supplier managed by the Operator:

- The Operator receives their share from the commission pool
- This applies even if another team member closes the deal

This system encourages Operators to treat supplier relationships as long-term business assets.

## Portfolio Accountability
Portfolio responsibility requires active management. It may be reviewed if:

- There is lack of communication with suppliers
- There is inactivity in company coordination
- Supplier product activity is not maintained
- Assigned supplier companies are not engaged

If issues occur and no reasonable explanation is provided, portfolio responsibility may be reassigned.

## Portfolio Transfer Rules
Supplier portfolio responsibility may be transferred in certain conditions:

1. **Operator exit**  
   If an Operator leaves the platform, their portfolio is reassigned.

2. **Supplier inactivity due to Operator**  
   If supplier inactivity is due to lack of Operator engagement, the portfolio may be reviewed and reassigned.

## Operator Success Formula
Successful Operators focus on three key areas:

1. **Relationship Building**  
   Develop strong and trusted relationships with supplier associates.

2. **Supplier Activation**  
   Ensure supplier products remain active and competitive.

3. **Market Awareness**  
   Stay connected with suppliers and share relevant market insights.

## Key Principle
The Operator role is not just about communication.  
It is about **supplier portfolio responsibility**.

Every supplier relationship is treated as a long-term trading asset that benefits both:

- The Operator
- The OBAOL platform
